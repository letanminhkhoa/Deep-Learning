Problem statement

Objective

The goal is to classify images into 5 semantic categories: animal, vehicle, person, food, indoor. Since the dataset is multimodal, both image and caption distributions must be analyzed.

Dataset Insights

Key Observations

  • Images mostly follow resolution ~640×480
  • Strong landscape bias in aspect ratio
  • Captions are short (8–12 words)
  • High frequency of "person"-related words

Distribution of image width and height.

Most images cluster around 640×480, indicating standardized data. This consistency helps reduce distortion during resizing and improves model stability.

Aspect ratio distribution.

Most images fall in the range 1.3–1.5 (landscape). Very few portrait images exist → dataset bias toward horizontal scenes.

⚠️ This suggests center cropping may lose important information → better to use TTA or random crops.

Caption length distribution.

Most captions contain 8–12 words, showing concise but meaningful descriptions.

Short captions → strong alignment with CLIP Long captions → richer context but introduce noise

Most frequent words in captions.

Words like “man”, “people”, “standing” dominate, indicating strong bias toward the Person category.

⚠️ This may cause the model to over-predict "Person".

Sample dataset images.

Many images contain multiple objects (e.g., person + food, person + animal).

⚠️ This introduces label ambiguity and makes classification more difficult.
