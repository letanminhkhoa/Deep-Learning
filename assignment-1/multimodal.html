EDA Observations

The dataset exhibits a significant class imbalance, where the person category dominates with over 5,000 samples, followed by animal and food. 03. Mean Feature Extraction Pipeline

Developing a multi-view representational strategy to ensure visual embedding stability against geometric shifts.

01

Multi-view Generation

For each image, I generate an ensemble of three spatial variants: the original image, a horizontal flip for mirror invariance, and a center-resized crop ($224 \times 224$).

02

CLIP Latent Projection

Each view is passed through the frozen CLIP ViT-B/32 Vision Encoder to extract 512-dimensional embedding vectors $\{v_1, v_2, v_3\}$.

03

L2 Normalization & Fusion

The final feature vector $\bar{v}$ is the normalized average of the ensemble, ensuring it sits on the unit hypersphere for valid cosine similarity.

$\bar{v} = \text{Normalize}\left(\frac{1}{3} \sum_{i=1}^{3} v_i\right)$

CLIP Architecture

CLIP contrastive learning

CLIP learns a shared embedding space between images and text using contrastive learning. Matching pairs are pulled closer while non-matching pairs are pushed apart.

At inference time, class labels are converted into prompts such as "a photo of a dog", and classification is performed via cosine similarity.

Performance Discussion

The Few-shot model achieves a significantly higher accuracy of 83.73% compared to the Zero-shot approach (65.07%), demonstrating the effectiveness of supervised adaptation even with noisy pseudo-labels. The confusion matrices reveal that while the Few-shot model correctly identifies most 'person' and 'animal' samples (644 and 249 respectively), the 'indoor' class remains a challenge for both paradigms due to visual ambiguity and smaller sample size. Zero-shot CLIP exhibits more confusion, particularly misclassifying 'person' as 'indoor' or 'vehicle' in complex scenes.

Key Insight: Label quality has a greater impact on performance than model complexity.

06. Project Extensions & Innovations

Innovation 01

LLM-based Semantic Distillation

Instead of basic keyword-matching, I implemented a zero-shot distillation pipeline using BART-Large-MNLI. This allows the project to handle abstract descriptions, assigning labels based on deep semantic context rather than brittle text overlaps.

Innovation 02

Multi-view Ensemble Extraction

I engineered a robust feature extraction strategy. By generating multiple geometric variants (original, flipped, resized) and averaging their embeddings, the model achieves mean feature stability, reducing sensitivity to spatial shifts.
